# Target Users and Personas

## AI Engineers
**Primary users** of the registry ecosystem. Develop AI applications, consume governed assets, create/contribute new assets.

**Interests**:
- Finding approved and usable AI assets
- Understanding asset metadata, status, compatibility
- Referencing governed assets during development
- Promoting assets through development workflows
- Understanding relationships between assets

**Registry value**: Makes AI assets easier to understand, trust, reuse, and promote.

## Platform Engineers
Responsible for setting up, integrating, and maintaining the AI platform environment.

**Interests**:
- Enabling registry capabilities across environments
- Integrating with OpenShift AI and platform services
- Supporting tenancy, access control, operational consistency
- Ensuring registry fits with APIs, K8s interfaces, gateways
- Maintaining scalable foundation for current and future asset types

**Registry value**: Clear, extensible framework that can be supported operationally.

## AgentOps Admins
Responsible for governance, approval, enablement, and operational oversight.

**Interests**:
- Reviewing and approving AI assets
- Controlling which assets are enabled for use
- Managing governance state and lifecycle progression
- Associating assets with policies and controls
- Understanding lineage, ownership, environment status
- Supporting promotion from dev to governed runtime contexts

**Registry value**: Strong governance capabilities, policy-aware workflows, clear operational view.

## Business Consumers
Indirect users who benefit from governed assets through applications, assistants, and services.

**Interests**:
- Access to trusted, approved AI capabilities
- Consistent, reliable user experiences
- Lower risk from ungoverned AI assets
- Confidence in governance backing AI solutions

**Registry value**: Safer, more manageable foundation for AI capabilities they consume.

## Key Customer Insights (from Agentic Strategy)

| Customer | Pain Point | Registry Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| SAS | Can't move agents to production without audit trail | Observability + governance |
| Amadeus | Need sophisticated identity model for multi-API agents | Agent identity + policy |
| Infineon | Multiple frameworks (AutoGen, LangChain, LangGraph) | Framework-agnostic registry |
| T-Mobile | No approved model registry, uncontrolled downloads | Centralized governance |
| Florida Blue | "DIY proliferation", need centralized control | Asset registry as control plane |
| Turkcell | Need project-level chargeback for agents | Cost tracking + observability |
